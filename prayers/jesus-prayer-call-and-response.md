For the prayer of confession, assurance, and thanksgiving, I would like to borrow from the broader Christian family, and have us pray together, the Jesus Prayer.

It goes like this:
”Lord Jesus Christ, Son of God, have mercy on me, a sinner.”

Before we say it together, a brief reflection:

**Confession:** We explicitly confess our state as sinners in need of mercy

”Lord Jesus Christ, Son of God, have mercy on me, a sinner.”

**Assurance:** Our bold request for mercy betrays our knowledge there is mercy for those who ask  through our Lord Jesus Christ

”Lord Jesus Christ, Son of God, have mercy on me, a sinner.”

**Thanksgiving:** Assumed, but not stated, heartfelt thanksgiving is our response as we receive God’s mercy

”Lord Jesus Christ, Son of God, have mercy on me, a sinner.”

So, on the count of three, **let’s confidently pray this prayer to our lavishly kind & generous God:**

”Lord Jesus Christ, Son of God, have mercy on me, a sinner.”
